The History of Mathematics:

An exciting journey to explore the intellectual horizon Mathematics is one of the oldest sciences that has continued to develop throughout the ages, and has formed an integral part of human thinking and the progress of society.

Here's an overview of the development of mathematics through the ages:

Antiquity:

Egyptians and Babylonians:
Mathematics began to appear in ancient times, when the Egyptians and Babylonians used it to solve life problems and to evaluate time using the hierarchical number system and the sexagesimal system.

Ancient Greece::

Archimedes and Euclid:
In the third century BC, Archimedes made important contributions to geometry and numbers, while Euclid established the foundations of analytical geometry.

middle ages:

Islamic mathematics:
Islamic mathematics greatly influenced scientific development. Islamic scholars made contributions to algebra and astronomical geometry, with important works such as Al-Khwarizmi's "The Book on the Calculation of Algebra and Muqabalah."

Renaissance era:

Rene Descartes:
In the seventeenth century, Ren√© Descartes established analytical geometry, combining algebra with geometry, which opened the horizon for major development in mathematics.

The modern era:

Gottfried Leibniz: In the eighteenth century, Leibniz introduced the concept of calculus, which contributed to the development of many branches of mathematics and their applications.

Contemporary era:

Computational mathematics and modern thinking:
With the development of computing, mathematics has moved into a new field, becoming an essential tool in understanding and analyzing complex phenomena.



The beauty of the history of mathematics lies in its continued development, and the great influence it has had on human thinking and the progress of society. The heritage of mathematics reflects the journey of exploring the intellectual horizon and the exciting challenges that man has faced throughout the ages
